⏰ at Commands (Deferred Tasks)
One-time future task scheduling with the at daemon
Click to copy
$at TIMESPEC
Schedule a deferred task at specified time
Press Ctrl+D to finish entering commands
Click to copy
$at now +5min < script.sh
Schedule script to run in 5 minutes using input redirection
job 3 at Wed May 18 21:03:00 2022
Click to copy
$at 02:00pm
Schedule task for 2:00 PM today (or tomorrow if time passed)
Natural time format - very flexible
Click to copy
$at teatime tomorrow
Schedule task for 4:00 PM (teatime) tomorrow
teatime = 16:00, supports many natural expressions
Click to copy
$atq
List pending at jobs (queue)
28 Mon May 16 05:13:00 2022 a user
29 Tue May 17 16:00:00 2022 h user
Click to copy
$at -l
Alternative command to list pending jobs
Equivalent to atq command
Click to copy
$at -c JOB_NUMBER
Display the commands in a scheduled job
echo "It's teatime" >> /home/user/tea.txt
Click to copy
$atrm JOB_NUMBER
Remove (cancel) a scheduled at job
Use job number from atq output
🕐 cron/crontab Commands
Recurring user task scheduling with crontab
Click to copy
$crontab -e
Edit user's crontab file
Opens vim editor by default (or $EDITOR)
Click to copy
$crontab -l
List user's current crontab entries
Shows all scheduled recurring jobs for current user
Click to copy
$crontab -r
Remove entire crontab file
WARNING: Deletes ALL user's cron jobs
Click to copy
#crontab -u username -e
Edit another user's crontab (root privilege required)
Only root can manage other users' crontabs
Click to copy
0 9 3 2 * /usr/local/bin/yearly_backup
Crontab entry: Run at 09:00 on February 3rd every year
Format: minute hour day-of-month month day-of-week command
Click to copy
*/5 9-16 * Jul 5 echo "Chime"
Every 5 minutes from 09:00-16:55 on Fridays in July
*/5 = every 5 minutes, 9-16 = hour range, 5 = Friday
Click to copy
58 23 * * 1-5 /usr/local/bin/daily_report
Daily report at 23:58 on weekdays (Monday-Friday)
1-5 = Monday through Friday range
Click to copy
0 9 * * 1-5 mutt -s "Checking in" user@example.com % Hi there
Send email at 9 AM weekdays with message body
% character treats following text as stdin input

🗂️ Temporary File Management
systemd-tmpfiles service for managing temporary files and directories
Click to copy
#systemd-tmpfiles --create
Create directories and files based on tmpfiles configuration

Click to copy
#systemd-tmpfiles --clean
Clean up old files based on age configuration
Removes files older than specified age
Click to copy
#systemd-tmpfiles --clean /etc/tmpfiles.d/tmp.conf

Process only specified configuration
Click to copy

q /tmp 1777 root root 5d

q=create dir, 1777=permissions, root=owner, 5d=age

d /run/momentary 0700 root root 30s

🕰️ Time Specifications
Time and date formats for at and cron commands
Click to copy
now +5min
Relative time: 5 minutes from now
at command natural time specification
Click to copy
teatime tomorrow
Named time: 16:00 (4 PM) tomorrow
teatime = 16:00, midnight = 00:00, noon = 12:00
Click to copy
noon +4 days
Combined: noon time, 4 days in future
Can combine named times with relative dates
Click to copy
5pm august 3 2021
Absolute date and time specification
Can use month names and years
Click to copy
* * * * *
Crontab format: minute hour day-of-month month day-of-week
* means "any value" for that field
Click to copy
*/15 * * * *
Crontab interval: every 15 minutes
*/N means "every N units" for that field
Click to copy
0 9-17 * * 1-5
Crontab ranges: top of hour, 9AM-5PM, weekdays
Ranges use dash (9-17), lists use commas (1,3,5)
Click to copy
15 12 11 * Fri
Crontab: 12:15 PM on 11th OR Fridays
Day-of-month AND day-of-week are OR condition
